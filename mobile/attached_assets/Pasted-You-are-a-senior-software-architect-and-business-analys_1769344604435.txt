You are a senior software architect and business analyst.

Your task is to design a formal Software Requirements Specification (SRS) for a mobile-first web application that manages a structured training program using self-enrollment, cell-based grouping, attendance tracking, assignment management, cash-based session payments, and graduation eligibility.

CONTEXT

The program operates in sessions. Participants and leaders self-register during a defined enrollment period. After enrollment closes, participants are grouped into balanced “cells” led by facilitators.

The system must reduce manual registers, WhatsApp coordination, and spreadsheet tracking while maintaining accountability and auditability.

USER ROLES

Define and clearly separate the following roles:

Participant

Self-register and onboard

View assigned cell and facilitator

View sessions, assignments, attendance count, payment status, and graduation eligibility

Submit assignments through the app

Cell Leader (Facilitator)

Self-register and await approval

Manage one assigned cell

Confirm attendance per session

Confirm cash payments per session

Confirm assignment completion

Validate graduation eligibility

Program Administrator

Read-only access to reports

No system configuration permissions

System Administrator

Full system access

Approve and assign Cell Leaders

Create enrollment periods and sessions

Trigger cell creation

Manually override cell assignments when necessary

Access all audit logs and reports

CORE FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

Include detailed sections for:

Enrollment periods and self-onboarding

Leader onboarding and approval

Session scheduling

Attendance tracking with facilitator confirmation

Assignment creation, submission, and confirmation

Cash-based payment tracking per session with no mobile or online payment integration

Graduation eligibility enforcement

CELL GROUPING RULES

Cells must:

Be created only after enrollment closes

Depend on the number of approved leaders and registered participants

Have configurable minimum and maximum sizes

Be balanced based on marital status, ensuring a fair distribution of married and unmarried participants

REQUIRED: EXACT CELL-BALANCING ALGORITHM

Write an explicit, step-by-step algorithm that:

Distributes participants evenly across cells

Balances married vs unmarried participants per cell

Handles uneven numbers gracefully

Enforces min and max cell sizes

Flags edge cases for administrator intervention

Allows manual overrides with audit logging

Use clear variable definitions and deterministic logic.

PAYMENT CONSTRAINTS

Payments are made per session

Payments are cash only

Facilitators record payments in the system

Payment records are immutable and auditable

Reports must reflect payment compliance

GRADUATION RULES

A participant is eligible to graduate only if:

They attended more than five (5) sessions

All assignments are submitted and confirmed

Attendance is confirmed by the facilitator

Participants with four (4) sessions or fewer must re-enroll in the next program cycle.

The system must automatically calculate and display eligibility status.

NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

Include:

Role-based access control

Audit logs for critical actions

Mobile-friendly design

Data integrity and validation

Admin override mechanisms with traceability
